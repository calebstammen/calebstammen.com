Stammen Technology: custom software, AI, and automation for businesses.

Stammen Technology began as a technology repair and IT solutions company and evolved into a custom software, web development, AI, automation, ecommerce, mobile app, and SaaS provider for businesses.

Overview

Stammen Technology is the company I use to take business problems seriously enough to build around them. The public site is business-facing, but the portfolio value is broader: it shows client-facing discovery, technical ownership, positioning, product judgment, and the discipline to deliver without a large team.

Evolution From Repair And IT To Software And AI

The business started with practical technology support and repair. Over time, the stronger lane became custom software, AI workflows, automation, ecommerce, websites, mobile applications, and SaaS foundations. That shift matches the problems businesses kept showing: not just broken devices, but broken workflows.

Technical Focus

  • Replacing spreadsheets, PDFs, calls, and memory-dependent processes with usable systems.
  • Connecting existing systems through APIs and practical integrations.
  • Building custom web apps, ecommerce systems, websites, mobile applications, and SaaS foundations.
  • Designing AI workflows where AI fits the process instead of becoming a disconnected demo.
  • Helping small and mid-sized businesses clarify intake, quoting, approvals, reporting, customer follow-up, and internal operations.

How The Work Usually Starts

The first useful question is not "what technology should we use?" It is "where does the work get slow or unclear?" From there, I map intake, handoffs, records, approvals, data, security, and the outputs people actually need.

  • Understand the current process and the real bottleneck.
  • Separate what needs software from what needs a clearer operating model.
  • Build the smallest durable system that removes meaningful friction.
  • Plan for launch, support, security, and future iteration.

What This Demonstrates

Stammen Technology shows entrepreneurship, client communication, business process analysis, practical software delivery, and the ability to move from vague operational pain to a scoped technical system.
